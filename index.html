About

Dr. Silvia Protopapa is a planetary astronomer at the South West Research Institute (SwRI). Her research focuses on understanding the physical and chemical properties of icy bodies in the outer Solar System, with a particular emphasis on data from the New Horizons mission to Pluto and beyond. Silvia combines laboratory spectroscopy, remote observations, and spacecraft data analysis to uncover the evolutionary history of Kuiper Belt objects.

Featured Projects

New Horizons Surface Composition

Mapping volatile ices across Pluto and Charon using Ralph/LEISA spectral observations to trace seasonal cycles and geological activity.

Kuiper Belt Object Spectroscopy

Coordinated laboratory and telescopic campaigns to identify spectral fingerprints of complex organics on small icy worlds.

Next-Generation Mission Concepts

Developing instrument concepts for future flyby and orbiter missions targeting dwarf planets and primordial Solar System bodies.
